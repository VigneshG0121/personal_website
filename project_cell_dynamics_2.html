Summary

This work was part of the MUST Intership program at the Mechanobiology Institute, National University of Singapore over the summer of 2025. I worked under Prof. Tsuyoshi Hirashima at the Collective Cell Dynamics Laboratory.

In this project, we explore the effect of cell deformability on ERK signalling using ERK-mediated Mechanochemically coupled Active Vertex model. Varying cell deformabilty was captured using Shape Parameter, a critical parameter in vertex models which controls the excitation energy required for T1 transitions and consequently the rigidity transition in confluent tissues. We observed that for a narrow range of the defined mechanochemical coupling ratio and shape parameter in the fluid domain, strong wavefronts of ERK signalling is formed. Furthermore, these strong wavefronts form through a series of transitional stanges and the range of parameters viable for the formation of strong wavefronts narrows down with increasing mechanochemical coupling ratio.
